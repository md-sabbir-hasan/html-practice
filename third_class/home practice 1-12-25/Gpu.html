About GPU

A Graphics Processing Unit (GPU) is a specialized electronic circuit designed to accelerate graphics and image processing by performing parallel calculations, originally for rendering images and video. GPUs are also used for a wide range of demanding tasks like gaming, scientific computing, machine learning, and artificial intelligence because of their ability to process many pieces of data simultaneously. They can be found on dedicated graphics cards or integrated directly into a computer's CPU.
